Saday Wellness — Cross-Analysis Audit

Document: docs/Saday_Cross_Analysis_Audit.html  ·  Date: 2026-05-05  ·  Subject: brutal audit of a second-Claude analysis (vendor-quote review + the founder's "cut DPDP, drop ASQ, add e-prescription, reduce tracks" proposal) cross-referenced against locked specs (data-model.md v1.1, intake-spec.md v1.0, stack-decision.md v1.0, Saday_Master_Blueprint) and the prior solo-agentic estimate (Saday_Solo_Agentic_Build_Estimate.html).
Goal: identify exactly which parts of the second-Claude analysis to trust, which to discard, and where the truth materially differs — across tech stack, scope, and cost (build · run · deploy · maintain) for both the planned (Next.js + Supabase) and proposed (Vue + Laravel + MySQL) paths.

The second-Claude analysis is mostly directionally correct and morally right where it matters most (refusing to drop DPDP and suicide screening, refusing to add e-prescription). It is weakest where it engages with your actual locked architecture — it underplays effort to make moral arguments, misses the agentic-codegen leverage angle, contradicts your spec without flagging the conflict, and contains at least one factually questionable claim (Laravel 13 non-existence). Use it as a sanity check against my prior analysis; do not adopt its scope-cut list verbatim.
